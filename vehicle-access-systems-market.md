<p><p>Vehicle Access Systems are categorized into two main types: Passive and Active systems. Passive Vehicle Access Systems use technologies like keyless entry and proximity sensors, allowing drivers to unlock and start their vehicles without physical keys, enhancing convenience and security. Active Vehicle Access Systems, on the other hand, require user engagement, such as pressing buttons or using remote key fobs to control vehicle entry and ignition. Both systems contribute to improving vehicle accessibility and user experience while offering varying levels of interaction.</p></p>
