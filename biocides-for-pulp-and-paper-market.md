<p><p>The biocides for the pulp and paper market are categorized into two main types: oxidizing and non-oxidizing biocides. Oxidizing biocides, such as chlorine dioxide and ozone, disrupt microbial metabolism through oxidative reactions, effectively controlling microbial growth and biofilm formation. Non-oxidizing biocides, like quaternary ammonium compounds and isothiazolinones, function by interfering with cellular processes and enzymes, ensuring prolonged protection against bacteria and fungi. Each type serves specific applications in pulp production and papermaking, enhancing product quality and operational efficiency.</p></p>
