<p><p>The spinal trauma device market is divided into internal and external devices. Internal devices include screws, plates, rods, and cages that are surgically implanted to stabilize the spine and facilitate healing. They are designed for severe spinal injuries requiring surgical intervention. External devices encompass braces and spinal orthotics used to support and immobilize the spine externally, aiding in recovery for less severe injuries. Both play crucial roles in enhancing patient outcomes in spinal trauma management.</p></p>
